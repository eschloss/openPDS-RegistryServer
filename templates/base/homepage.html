What is it?

The openPDS Registry Server contains a registry of users with personal data stores, and provides these users with an OAuth 2.0 service to authorize applications to utilize their PDS.

The Philosophy

You created your personal data - whether it was by filling out a form, wearing a quantified-self device, or running one of the many mobile applications available today that collect information about you, including call logs, your location, and your contacts.

  1. This data belongs to you
  2. You should know where it lives
  3. You should know how it is used
  4. You should be able to use all of it across all of your apps and sites
Unfortunately, the status-quo has many applications collecting your personal data, with strict terms of service that essentially hand over ownership to the company collecting it in exchange for the service they are providing. Additionally, there is seldom any mention of how they are really using your data, and where they are housing it - there is typically not even a link to view the data these services have on you. Finally, all of these applications store their data separately and thus, no one application has access to all of your data. Your music app can't use the data from your excercise app to help determine the perfect song to play, and your mapping application can't use data from your social networking application to help determine what restaurants your friends like. If you knew where all of your data lived, and could provide access to all of it accross applications, these applications could potentially do even more for you.
